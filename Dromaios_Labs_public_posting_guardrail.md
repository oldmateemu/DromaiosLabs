# Dromaios Labs Public Posting Guardrail

Compact guardrail for LinkedIn, owned articles, website snippets, founder posts, and short public updates that reference Dromaios Labs, ClinicBoss, Bakermed, or future medtech.

This is a practical content-control note, not legal, trademark, patent, or TGA advice. Update it after the provisional patent, formal trademark clearance, and TGA/SaMD assessment are complete.

## Current Public Direction

Dromaios Labs should keep the active direction: authority and trust first, healthcare leaders and managers first, LinkedIn plus owned articles first, Dromaios Labs as the umbrella, company-led with a human founder voice, ClinicBoss visible but careful, and medtech explicit but measured.

The safe public frame is:

> Dromaios Labs is building practical healthcare infrastructure across education, tools, operational software, and responsible medtech, with claims kept close to real evidence.

## Source Risk Reconciliation

The Bakermed docs create three hard content constraints:

1. Patent disclosure risk: treat any feature-level detail about ClinicBoss or future medtech as confidential until a provisional patent is filed. Avoid public explanations of unique workflows, algorithms, architecture, screenshots, demo flows, data models, or implementation methods.
2. Trademark-clearance risk: treat "ClinicBoss" as a working product name until clearance is complete. Use it sparingly, avoid heavy brand investment in public copy, and keep backup-name flexibility.
3. TGA/SaMD overclaiming risk: public language can help define intended use. Do not claim that ClinicBoss or future Dromaios medtech diagnoses, treats, monitors, predicts, recommends, improves clinical outcomes, or replaces professional judgement unless the regulatory pathway and evidence support that claim.

## Green, Amber, Red

### Green: publishable without special review

- Dromaios Labs as an Australia-first healthcare innovation umbrella.
- Broad problems: safety, compliance pressure, documentation burden, workflow clarity, implementation friction, and operational reliability.
- Education, tools, and operational software as practical proof streams.
- ClinicBoss as a working product stream focused on operational and compliance workflows.
- Future medtech as a long-term direction, approached carefully and evidence-first.
- Founder learning, principles, and observations at category level.

### Amber: review before posting

- Any mention of specific ClinicBoss modules, workflows, screenshots, demos, or "how it works."
- Any use of "ClinicBoss" as if the trademark is final.
- Any pricing, launch timing, pilots, customer names, traction, savings, or outcome numbers.
- Any comparison with named competitors.
- Any "clinical", "AI", "decision support", "risk prediction", "triage", "monitoring", or "recommendation" language.

### Red: do not publish yet

- "ClinicBoss reduces no-shows by X%", "saves X hours", "improves patient outcomes", or "clinically validated" unless measured and approved for release.
- "Compliant by design", "TGA-ready", "TGA-approved", "SaMD-ready", or "not a medical device" before the assessment is documented.
- "Diagnoses", "treats", "predicts", "monitors", "recommends treatment", "clinical decision support", or "medical advice."
- Feature-level patentable detail, architecture diagrams, workflow diagrams, product screenshots, demo videos, or algorithmic explanations before the patent position is settled.
- ClinicBoss domains, logos, campaigns, or brand-heavy announcements before trademark clearance.

## Default Posting Formula

Use this structure for most public posts:

1. Start with a real healthcare operational problem.
2. Connect it to Dromaios Labs' practical infrastructure direction.
3. Mention ClinicBoss only as a careful product stream, if needed.
4. Keep future medtech as an earned direction, not a current claim.
5. Avoid feature mechanics, clinical claims, outcome numbers, and final-brand certainty.

Safe wording patterns:

- "a working product stream"
- "operational software"
- "healthcare compliance and workflow problems"
- "built close to real healthcare work"
- "responsible medtech direction"
- "we are learning through education, tools, and operational systems"
- "designed to support teams, not replace professional judgement"

Avoid unless approved:

- "the ClinicBoss platform"
- "our patented method"
- "clinically validated"
- "diagnoses"
- "AI clinical decision support"
- "TGA-ready"
- "compliant by design"
- "proven outcomes"
- "trusted by clinics"

## Five Safe Example Messages

### 1. Dromaios umbrella post

Healthcare innovation should earn trust before it asks for it.

Dromaios Labs is being built around that principle: start with practical education, tools, and operational software, then let the long-term medtech direction grow from real workflow learning.

The work is not about making the loudest claim. It is about understanding where healthcare teams face safety, compliance, documentation, and implementation pressure, then building carefully from there.

### 2. ClinicBoss mention, careful and non-final

One Dromaios Labs product stream, currently referred to as ClinicBoss, is focused on healthcare compliance and operational workflows.

I am being deliberate about how I talk about it publicly: the important story right now is not a big product promise, but the operational problem underneath it.

Healthcare teams need systems that support clear work, better evidence trails, and safer service delivery without pretending software replaces clinical judgement.

### 3. Future medtech direction

Medtech is the long-term direction for Dromaios Labs, but I do not think serious healthcare technology should jump straight to grand claims.

The path I am taking is more deliberate: education to understand safety and workforce needs, practical tools to test usefulness, operational software to learn real workflows, and only then broader medtech built with the right evidence and governance around it.

That slower path is the point.

### 4. Healthcare leaders and managers

Most healthcare leaders are not short on buzzwords. They are short on practical systems that hold up under pressure.

That is the space Dromaios Labs is focused on: safety, compliance habits, workflow clarity, documentation, and tools that fit the operational reality of care.

The goal is simple to say and hard to build: practical infrastructure for safer, better-run healthcare.

### 5. Founder voice

I am trying to build Dromaios Labs in public without overclaiming.

That means talking openly about the problems we are studying: safety pressure, compliance burden, operational gaps, and the difficulty of building healthcare technology responsibly.

It also means being careful about product detail, evidence, and regulatory language. In healthcare, credibility is part of the product.

## Quick Pre-Post Check

Before publishing, ask:

- Does this reveal how ClinicBoss or future medtech works in a way that could matter for patent protection?
- Does it make "ClinicBoss" look like a cleared final brand?
- Does it imply diagnosis, treatment, monitoring, prediction, clinical decision support, medical advice, or patient-outcome improvement?
- Does it imply customer traction, adoption, validation, savings, or regulatory status that is not documented?
- Could this same message survive a lawyer, patent attorney, trademark agent, TGA consultant, and healthcare customer reading it side by side?

If any answer is yes or uncertain, soften the post back to problem-level, principle-level, or umbrella-brand language.
