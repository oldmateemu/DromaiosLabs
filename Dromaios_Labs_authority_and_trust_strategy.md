# Dromaios Labs Authority and Trust Strategy

How Dromaios Labs becomes a recognised, authoritative healthcare company in Australia
without overclaiming, and how it keeps trust while standing out.

This is a strategy and sequencing note, not legal, regulatory, or financial advice. It
complements two existing documents and should be read alongside them:

- `Dromaios_Labs_company_statements.md` covers the voice (what we say).
- `Dromaios_Labs_public_posting_guardrail.md` covers the claims guardrail (what we must
  not say yet).

This document covers the gap between them: the **authority-and-trust engine** that earns
the standing to be believed. The existing docs tell us how to speak safely. They do not
yet say how we build the credibility that makes the speaking land. That is the unmarked
territory.

## The Core Idea

In healthcare, **credibility is part of the product**. Buyers, clinicians, and regulators
do not reward the loudest claim; they reward the company that is visibly safe to trust
with risk. So the route to "recognised and authoritative" is not louder marketing. It is
**accumulating trust signals faster than competitors while keeping every claim behind its
evidence**.

Authority = (visible proof of doing the work) + (formal trust signals) + (consistent,
careful public voice), compounded over time.

We already have the third. This document builds the first two.

## What Counts as "Unmarked Territory"

Against the current docs, these areas are not yet planned:

1. **Trust infrastructure** - the formal credentials a healthcare buyer or regulator looks
   for (clinical governance, security/privacy certification, quality systems, standards
   alignment).
2. **The authority engine** - how recognition is actually manufactured (evidence
   publication, thought-leadership cadence, industry membership, awards, accelerators,
   grants).
3. **Differentiation that is safe** - the defensible wedge that lets us stand out without
   tripping the claims guardrail.
4. **Trust-retention and downside control** - how we avoid losing trust (breach
   readiness, conflict-of-interest, claim-to-evidence discipline).
5. **A recognition roadmap** - staged proof points from "unknown" to "authority", with
   leading indicators so we know it is working.

Each is below.

## 1. Trust Infrastructure (the credentials that unlock the room)

These are the signals procurement teams, clinical leads, and partners check before they
take you seriously. They are slow to earn, which is exactly why they create a moat. Build
them in the order that matches each stream's actual exposure - do not buy certifications
you do not yet need.

### Security and privacy (earn early, it gates everything)

The moment ClinicBoss touches real practice or client data, security posture becomes the
first question every buyer asks. ISO 27001 is now effectively the **baseline expectation
for technology vendors serving Australian healthcare**, and it underpins compliance with
the Privacy Act 1988, the My Health Records Act 2012, and the Notifiable Data Breaches
scheme.

Practical ladder:

- **Now / cheap:** adopt the ASD **Essential Eight** controls and document them. This is
  the credible starting posture and maps directly onto ISO 27001 Annex A later.
- **Before first real pilot:** a written information security policy, data-handling and
  breach-response plan, and a Privacy Impact Assessment habit. These are "show on request"
  artefacts that win trust cheaply.
- **When selling to organisations:** pursue **ISO 27001** (and ISO 27701 for privacy).
  Treat it as a sales enabler, not a vanity badge.
- **Only if/when targeting government or hospital systems:** **IRAP** assessment against
  the ISM. ISO 27001 gets you roughly 40-60% of the way there, so sequence it after.

This is the single highest-leverage trust investment and the one most startups defer too
long. In our case it doubles as proof of the "responsible" value.

### Clinical governance (the signal that we are safe near care)

Recognition in healthcare comes faster with credible clinical voices attached. Establish,
in rough order:

- A small **clinical/safety advisory group** (named clinicians, aged/community-care
  operators, a compliance or quality lead). Even 2-3 advisors changes how we are read.
- A written **clinical safety and claims policy** - the internal twin of the public
  posting guardrail - stating that we sequence claims to evidence and never imply
  diagnosis, treatment, or outcome improvement without the pathway to support it.
- Clear **intended-use statements** for each product stream. Under TGA rules, public
  language helps define intended use, so this protects both safety and regulatory standing.

### Quality and regulatory readiness (stage to the medtech direction)

Do not carry medtech-grade overhead before the medtech work needs it. But signpost the
path, because saying "we know the road" is itself an authority signal:

- **NSQHS Standards** (Australian Commission on Safety and Quality in Health Care) for any
  work touching clinical services.
- **Aged Care Quality Standards** under the new **Aged Care Act 2024** (in effect 1
  November 2025) for aged/community-care work - a live, high-attention area worth aligning
  to early.
- For the medtech direction specifically, the future quality stack is **ISO 13485**
  (quality management), **IEC 62304** (software lifecycle), and **ISO 14971** (risk
  management). Name these as the destination; do not build them yet.
- Track the **TGA Software-as-a-Medical-Device** framework, including the AI-specific
  guidance the TGA issued in February 2026 following its 2025 report on safe use of AI in
  healthcare, and the **clinical-decision-support exemptions/exclusions**. Knowing exactly
  where ClinicBoss sits (regulated, exempt, or excluded) is both a safety control and a
  credibility differentiator. Most competitors are vague here; precision stands out.

## 2. The Authority Engine (how recognition is manufactured)

Trust infrastructure makes us *safe* to trust. The authority engine makes us *known*. The
posting guardrail already governs the safety of each post; what is missing is the
**cadence and the channels** that compound into recognition.

### Evidence and publication ladder

Move up this ladder as proof accumulates. Each rung is more authoritative and harder to
fake - that is the point.

1. **Field observations** (now): category-level founder learning about safety, compliance
   burden, documentation, and workflow friction. Already green-lit by the guardrail.
2. **Structured insight pieces**: owned long-form articles and short whitepapers framing a
   problem and a principled approach - no product mechanics, no outcome numbers.
3. **Anonymised case learning**: once pilots run, "what we learned" write-ups with the
   numbers redacted until measured and approved.
4. **Co-authored / endorsed work**: pieces written with an advisor or partner organisation
   attached. Borrowed credibility, honestly earned.
5. **Formal evidence**: eventually, measured pilot results and, where it fits the medtech
   path, peer-reviewed or grey-literature publication.

The discipline: **publish one rung above where most people would dare, and one rung below
what the evidence can defend.** That gap is where credible-but-bold lives.

### Cadence (the part that actually builds recognition)

Recognition is a frequency game played at low risk. A workable rhythm:

- **Weekly:** one founder/company post at field-observation or principle level (guardrail
  green). Consistency matters more than brilliance.
- **Monthly:** one deeper owned article or whitepaper (rung 2-3).
- **Quarterly:** one "earned" signal - a talk, a partner piece, an award entry, a grant
  application, an advisor announcement, or a certification milestone.

The cockpit's automation/approval model is the right place to run this as a draft-then-
approve pipeline, keeping every item inside the guardrail.

### Channels and rooms to be in

Being recognised means being recognised *by the people who confer recognition*. Targets:

- **Industry bodies / community:** Medical Software Industry Association (MSIA), AusBiotech,
  ANDHealth (digital health commercialisation), and aged/community-care peak bodies (e.g.
  the Aged & Community Care Providers Association). Membership and visible participation are
  themselves trust signals.
- **Accelerators:** ANDHealth's digital-health programs are the most relevant Australian
  pathway and confer real credibility plus introductions.
- **Awards and lists:** enter the categories we can honestly win (founder, emerging
  company, safety/education). An entry is cheap; a shortlist is an authority asset.
- **Grants as credibility, not just cash:** the **MRFF 2026 BioMedTech Incubator** stream
  (opened Feb 2026; SME digital-health pathway exists) and the broader Medical Research
  Commercialisation initiative. A competitive grant is third-party validation as much as
  funding. Note BioMedTech Horizons is closed to new funding - target the live programs.
- **Speaking and panels:** education and safety topics first (where we have the most
  standing), medtech topics last.

## 3. Differentiation That Is Safe

The hard part: stand out without the language the guardrail forbids. The differentiation
must come from **posture and proof, not product claims**. Three defensible wedges:

1. **"Built close to the work."** Most healthtech is built far from the workflow and
   over-promises. Our wedge is the opposite: education and operational learning *first*,
   product claims *last*. This is already in the statements - elevate it from a tagline to
   the central proof of how we operate, and show it (the proof-stream ladder is the
   evidence).
2. **Trust as the visible feature.** Make the *carefulness itself* the differentiator:
   "we keep claims behind evidence", "designed to support judgement, not replace it",
   "regulatory-aware by design". In a market full of inflated claims, audible restraint is
   distinctive and quietly authoritative.
3. **The proof-stream ladder as a moat.** Education -> tools -> operational software ->
   medtech is not just a roadmap; it is a credibility compounding machine no
   single-product competitor can copy quickly. Each stream feeds the next with real
   workflow learning. Tell that story - it is differentiating *and* fully guardrail-safe.

What we explicitly do **not** differentiate on (per the guardrail): outcome numbers,
clinical capability, AI cleverness, "platform" scale, or named-customer traction - until
each is earned and approved.

## 4. Trust-Retention and Downside Control

Authority is lost faster than it is built. The asymmetry means downside control is part of
the strategy, not an afterthought.

- **Claim-to-evidence discipline:** the existing guardrail's pre-post check is the primary
  defence. Keep it. The failure mode that destroys healthcare credibility is a claim that
  outran its evidence.
- **Breach readiness:** have a Notifiable-Data-Breaches response plan *before* handling
  real data. A handled incident preserves trust; a fumbled one ends it.
- **Conflict-of-interest and independence:** when advisors or partners are involved,
  disclose relationships. Transparency is a trust multiplier.
- **Consistency across audiences:** the guardrail's "could this survive a lawyer, patent
  attorney, TGA consultant, and customer reading it side by side" test should extend to
  every channel, including talks and grant applications, not just posts.
- **Quiet on the unproven:** silence on what we have not yet earned is a feature. Saying
  less than we could is itself a trust signal in this market.

## 5. Recognition Roadmap (staged proof points)

A sequencing ladder from unknown to authority. Phases are gates, not calendar dates - move
up only when the prior rung's proof exists.

### Phase 0 - Foundations (credible from day one)

- Essential Eight posture documented; security/privacy/breach plans drafted.
- 1-2 clinical/safety advisors named.
- Intended-use statements written for each stream.
- Weekly green-level posting cadence running through the cockpit.

Leading indicators: consistent posting streak; first advisor confirmed; first inbound
"tell me more" from a healthcare leader.

All four phases are operationalised in the cockpit: they are seeded as tracked `Action`
records (source USER, status OPEN), each tagged with its phase, from
`src/lib/strategy-checklist.ts`, so every item appears on the Today board and Actions
register and moves through the normal action lifecycle and weekly governance review.
Re-seeding is idempotent and will not duplicate items. Treat later-phase items as the
backlog: they become live only when the prior phase's proof exists.

### Phase 1 - Credible presence (recognised as serious)

- Membership in 1-2 industry bodies; visible participation.
- Monthly owned articles (rung 2-3) accumulating into a body of work.
- First grant or accelerator application submitted (credibility regardless of outcome).
- DromaiosEd delivering real education - the fastest-to-authority stream because it
  requires no product claims.

Leading indicators: growing owned-content library; first speaking invite or panel; first
partner conversation; grant/accelerator shortlist.

### Phase 2 - Earned authority (sought out)

- ISO 27001 in progress or achieved; security used as a sales enabler.
- First pilots running; anonymised case learning published (rung 3).
- An award shortlist or competitive grant won.
- ClinicBoss spoken about with slightly more confidence, still inside the guardrail.

Leading indicators: inbound partnership/pilot requests; media or peak-body references to
us; repeat speaking; advisory group expanded.

### Phase 3 - Recognised company (the standing to lead the conversation)

- Measured, approved evidence published; medtech direction discussed with the quality and
  regulatory path visibly underway.
- Reference customers/partners willing to be named.
- Recognised voice in safety/compliance/operational-care conversations.

Leading indicators: others cite our framing; clinicians and operators seek our view;
regulators/peak bodies treat us as an informed party.

## How to Use This Document

- Treat Phase 0 as immediately actionable; most of it is cheap and compounding.
- Run the authority-engine cadence as draft-then-approve work inside the cockpit, governed
  by the existing posting guardrail.
- Revisit the trust-infrastructure ladder whenever a stream's data exposure or buyer type
  changes - that is the trigger for the next certification.
- Update this note as the provisional patent, trademark clearance, and TGA/SaMD assessment
  complete, in step with the posting guardrail's own update trigger.
