# Customer Acquisition Strategy

How Dromaios Labs gets the right healthcare buyers through the door and purchasing,
without overclaiming and without putting ClinicBoss IP, trademark, or TGA position at risk.

This strategy is deliberately built around what is **sellable now** (education and practical
tools) and treats ClinicBoss and medtech as trust-builders and pipeline, not the opening offer.
It maps directly to the streams in `docs/OPERATING_MODEL.md` and obeys
`Dromaios_Labs_public_posting_guardrail.md`.

---

## 1. The core idea

Healthcare buyers do not purchase from unproven medtech promises. They purchase from people
who clearly understand their operational reality and have already helped someone like them.

So the acquisition motion is **authority first, education as the front door, software as the
earned next step.**

```
ATTRACT            CONVERT              PURCHASE (front door)     EXPAND
Authority content  Free practical       Paid education /          ClinicBoss design
on LinkedIn +      resource + a real    safety + compliance       partner, tools,
owned articles     conversation         training (DromaiosEd)     and medtech direction
```

The job of every early customer is twofold: revenue from education today, and a real
healthcare relationship that becomes a ClinicBoss pilot / design partner tomorrow.

## 2. Who we are selling to (ICP)

From the company statements, the buyers are healthcare **leaders and managers responsible for
safe service delivery.** Prioritise by who can say yes fastest and feels the pain hardest.

### Tier 1 — best first customers (lead here)
- **Aged-care providers** (residential + home/community care): high compliance pressure,
  workforce safety and de-escalation needs, ongoing training obligations, real budgets.
- **Community-care and disability-care providers**: frontline safety, lone-worker risk,
  documentation burden, audit pressure.
- **Multi-site clinic groups / practice managers**: compliance habits, onboarding, workflow
  clarity across sites.

### Tier 2 — strong, slightly longer cycle
- Allied health groups and primary care networks.
- Training/RTO partners and industry associations (channel partners, not just buyers).

### The person who signs
- Operations / quality / compliance lead, clinical manager, L&D / workforce lead,
  practice principal or owner.

### What they are actually buying
Not "innovation." They are buying **fewer incidents, smoother audits, faster onboarding,
calmer teams, and evidence they can show a regulator.** Sell that.

## 3. The offer ladder (the path to "purchasing")

People come through the door at low friction and climb. Each rung funds and de-risks the next.

| Rung | Offer | Price posture | Purpose |
|------|-------|---------------|---------|
| 0 | Free practical resource (checklist, short guide, self-assessment) | Free | Capture + permission to follow up |
| 1 | Paid workshop / short course — safety, de-escalation, compliance habits | Low, easy yes | First purchase, proof of value |
| 2 | Team / multi-session program + delivery on site or virtual | Mid | Recurring revenue, deeper relationship |
| 3 | Annual education + practical tools (HIL/Skool resources) bundle | Subscription | Predictable revenue, expansion |
| 4 | ClinicBoss design-partner / pilot | Pilot-priced or partnered | Earned via trust; pipeline to software revenue |

**Rule:** never open with rung 4. ClinicBoss enters the conversation only as "a working product
stream" once the relationship exists (see guardrail). Education is the cash-flow engine and the
trust engine simultaneously.

## 4. The acquisition channels (in priority order)

The guardrail already sets the channel direction: **LinkedIn + owned articles first,
authority and trust first.** Concretely:

### 4.1 LinkedIn (primary engine)
- **Founder voice + company page.** The founder posts; the company amplifies.
- 3 posts/week using the guardrail's "Default Posting Formula": start with a real operational
  problem → connect to practical infrastructure → keep claims close to evidence.
- Post types that pull buyers:
  - "Here's a safety/compliance problem I keep seeing in [aged care / community care]…"
  - Short frameworks and checklists (give value away).
  - Founder learning-in-public notes (build the human, accountable voice).
- **Engage, don't just broadcast:** comment on Tier 1 leaders' posts daily. Connection requests
  to ICP with a genuine note, not a pitch.

### 4.2 Owned articles / email list
- One longer practical article per fortnight on the site, repurposed into LinkedIn.
- Every free resource is gated by email so attention becomes a list you own (not rented).
- Light, useful fortnightly email: one problem, one practical tip, one soft CTA.

### 4.3 Direct, warm outreach (the underrated one)
- Personal, researched outreach to 10–15 Tier 1 orgs/week. Lead with a relevant insight or the
  free self-assessment — **not** a demo request.
- Goal of outreach is one thing only: a 20-minute discovery conversation (see §5 framing).

### 4.4 Partnerships / channel
- Industry associations, peak bodies, and RTOs that already have the audience.
- Co-run a free webinar; they bring the room, Dromaios brings the practical content.
- This is the fastest way to "get customers through the door" in volume.

### 4.5 Events / speaking
- Pitch to speak at aged-care, community-care, and practice-management forums.
- One good talk = a room full of Tier 1 buyers + content for months.

> Paid ads are intentionally **not** an early channel. This buyer trusts authority and referral
> far more than ads, and ads can't carry the careful claims posture.

## 5. The conversion path (door → purchase)

The "discovery call framing" in the company statements is exactly right. Make it a repeatable funnel:

1. **Attract** — content/outreach surfaces a real problem the buyer recognises.
2. **Capture** — they take the free resource → on the email list, identified.
3. **Converse** — book a 20-min discovery call. Purpose: *understand the operational problem
   first*, not pitch. Qualify against ICP and budget.
4. **Recommend** — propose the smallest useful next step: usually a paid workshop or short
   course (rung 1/2). "A good pilot should be narrow, useful, and testable."
5. **Purchase** — simple, fast checkout / invoice for education. Remove friction: fixed packages,
   clear price, clear outcome, easy yes.
6. **Deliver well** — over-deliver on the first paid engagement. First impressions become referrals.
7. **Expand** — after proof, introduce team programs, tools, and (only when earned) a ClinicBoss
   design-partner conversation.

**Conversion principle:** make the *first* purchase tiny and obvious. Revenue compounds through
delivery quality and expansion, not through a big first sale.

## 6. Proof and trust assets (what makes them say yes)

Buyers in healthcare buy credibility. Build a small library that does the convincing for you:

- **Self-assessment / scorecard** (e.g. "Workforce safety & compliance readiness") — doubles as
  the free lead magnet and a discovery tool.
- **Practical one-pagers**: de-escalation basics, documentation that survives an audit,
  onboarding safety checklist.
- **Case notes / outcomes** — once real, but stated carefully (no unmeasured savings/outcome claims
  per the Red list). "What we did, what the team reported."
- **Founder POV articles** that demonstrate operational depth.
- **Testimonials** from early education clients (social proof is allowed; outcome numbers are not,
  unless measured and approved).

## 7. Guardrail compliance (non-negotiable)

Every acquisition asset must pass the guardrail's pre-post check. In practice:

- **Lead with education and practical safety/compliance** (Green). That's the whole opening offer.
- **ClinicBoss** appears only as "a working product stream" / "operational software." No modules,
  screenshots, demos, "how it works," pricing, traction, or final-brand certainty (Amber/Red).
- **No clinical / outcome / TGA claims**: nothing about diagnosing, monitoring, predicting,
  recommending, "clinically validated," "saves X hours/%," or "compliant by design" unless measured
  and approved.
- When in doubt, **soften to problem-level / principle-level / umbrella-brand language.**

This protects the patent, trademark, and TGA position while still selling hard on education.

## 8. Operating cadence (wire it into the cockpit)

This maps onto the Daily/Weekly rhythm in `docs/OPERATING_MODEL.md` so acquisition is a system,
not a mood.

**Daily (in the Today Command Board)**
- Publish or engage: 1 LinkedIn action.
- 3–5 warm outreach touches to Tier 1.
- Reply to every inbound within the day.

**Weekly (in the Weekly Review — sales + marketing functions)**
- Review pipeline: leads in, conversations booked, proposals out, purchases closed.
- Ship 1 owned article + repurpose to 3 LinkedIn posts.
- Advance 1 partnership conversation.
- Review metrics (§9) and pick next week's focus.

**Monthly**
- 1 webinar or talk (own or partner audience).
- Refresh one proof asset.
- Review ICP fit: which Tier-1 segment is converting best → double down.

---

## 9. Metrics (how we know it's working)

Keep it to a small scoreboard. Track in the cockpit.

**Leading indicators (do these and revenue follows)**
- LinkedIn posts/week and meaningful comments/week.
- New email subscribers / resource downloads.
- Warm outreach touches/week.
- Discovery conversations booked/week.

**Conversion indicators**
- Conversation → proposal rate.
- Proposal → first-purchase rate.
- Time from first touch to first purchase.

**Revenue + expansion**
- New education customers / month and revenue.
- Repeat + expansion rate (rung 1 → rung 2/3).
- Number of qualified ClinicBoss design-partner conversations created (pipeline, not a sales target yet).

**Early targets are deliberately about activity and learning, not big revenue.** Get the engine
turning, measure conversion, then scale the channels that convert.

## 10. 90-day plan

### Days 1–30 — Foundations & first conversations
- Define the single sharpest Tier-1 segment to lead with (recommend: aged care **or** community care).
- Build the free self-assessment + 2–3 one-pagers (lead magnets).
- Stand up the email capture + simple fortnightly email.
- Start the LinkedIn cadence (3/week) and daily engagement.
- Begin warm outreach (10–15 ICP orgs/week) → aim for 5–8 discovery conversations.
- Package rung-1 education offer with fixed price and clear outcome.

### Days 31–60 — First revenue & repeatability
- Convert discovery conversations into first paid education engagements.
- Deliver exceptionally; gather testimonials and careful case notes.
- Lock in 1 partnership/webinar with a peak body or RTO.
- Publish first 2 owned articles; keep the LinkedIn engine running.
- Tighten the funnel: measure conversation→proposal→purchase rates.

### Days 61–90 — Scale what converts & open expansion
- Double down on the best-converting channel + segment.
- Introduce rung-2/3 (team programs + tools bundle) to happy customers.
- Run the first webinar to a partner audience for volume.
- Begin **earned** ClinicBoss design-partner conversations with the most engaged customers
  (problem-level only, per guardrail).
- Review the scoreboard and set the next 90-day targets from real conversion data.

---

## 11. Summary

- **Sell education now** — it's the credible, compliant, cash-generating front door.
- **Lead with authority** on LinkedIn + owned articles + warm outreach + partnerships.
- **Make the first purchase tiny and obvious**, then expand through great delivery.
- **Use every education customer** as the trust bridge to ClinicBoss design partners and the
  long-term medtech direction.
- **Never overclaim.** In healthcare, credibility is part of the product — and it's also the
  acquisition strategy.

Public-facing copy is checked mechanically against `Dromaios_Labs_public_posting_guardrail.md`:

  covering Red detection, Amber surfacing, ignore markers, line accuracy, and a scan of every
  shipped public-content file.
- **Cadence:** run the linter before any public content ships (see the operating cadence checklist).
