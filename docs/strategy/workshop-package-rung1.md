# Rung-1 Offer — Paid Workshop Package: "The First 30 Seconds"

The first paid purchase in the offer ladder (`CUSTOMER_ACQUISITION_STRATEGY.md` §3, rung 1).
Designed to be an **easy yes**: fixed scope, clear outcome, low friction, deliverable virtually or
on-site. Its job is to convert a discovery conversation into revenue *and* a great first delivery
experience that earns referrals and expansion.

**Guardrail:** the workshop builds practical capability. It makes no clinical, outcome, or
regulatory-status claims. We describe what participants will be able to *do*, not patient outcomes
or guaranteed metrics. Don't promise "reduces incidents by X."

---

## The offer in one line
A practical half-day workshop that turns your team's safety policies into rehearsed habits — what to
say, do, and document in the moments that actually decide how an incident goes.

## Who it's for
Aged-care, community-care, and disability-care teams — frontline staff, team leaders, and the
managers who support them. Best run as an intact team (people who actually work together).
*(Swap segment language as needed.)*

## The outcome (what they walk away able to do)
By the end, the team can:
- Respond in the first 30 seconds of an escalating situation using a shared, rehearsed playbook.
- Document an incident same-day in a consistent, defensible shape.
- Name who owns each key safety/compliance area, so nothing sits with "everyone/no one."
- Run their *own* 30-minute scenario rehearsal after we leave (so it sticks).

---

## Format & logistics
- **Length:** 3.5 hours (half day). Optional full-day version adds deeper scenario practice + a
  compliance-habits module.
- **Delivery:** on-site or live virtual.
- **Group size:** up to 20 (keeps scenario practice workable). Larger teams → multiple sessions.
- **What we bring:** facilitation, scenario cards, the documentation shape template, and a take-home
  one-pager per participant.
- **What they provide:** a room/screen, the team's real (de-identified) scenario types, and a
  manager in the room (buy-in matters).

## Run sheet (3.5-hour version)

| Time | Segment | What happens |
|------|---------|--------------|
| 0:00–0:20 | Why the first 30 seconds | The pattern: incidents are decided before anyone realises. Sets the frame, gets buy-in. |
| 0:20–1:05 | Build the playbook | Team picks their 4–5 most common escalation scenarios and answers: what we *say* / *do* / *call*. Written in plain words. |
| 1:05–1:20 | Break | |
| 1:20–2:05 | Rehearse it | Low-drama walk-throughs of the scenarios. Practise until the response is a reflex, not a recall. |
| 2:05–2:45 | Documentation that survives scrutiny | The same-day habit + the consistent shape (what happened / what was done / follow-up). Practise on a real-style scenario. |
| 2:45–3:10 | Ownership & habits | Name an owner per key area; agree the team's daily safety/compliance habits. |
| 3:10–3:30 | Make it stick | Teach the team to run their own 30-minute rehearsal. Agree the next one's date before we leave. |

**Optional full-day add-ons:** lone/community-worker safety process, onboarding-readiness for new
starters, and a deeper compliance-habits clinic.

  bridge to rung 2: a team program).

## How it converts up the ladder
- **Rung 1 → 2:** at the close, offer a follow-up session in 6–8 weeks to embed and extend
  (turns one-off into a program).
- **Rung 2 → 3:** bundle ongoing refreshers + practical tools (HIL/Skool resources) into an annual
  arrangement.
- **Trust → ClinicBoss:** for engaged clients with a clear operational-software pain, *only then*
  open a problem-level conversation about the ClinicBoss design-partner direction. Never in the
  workshop pitch itself.
