# Pathway 07 — Corporatised High-Compliance Niches: Diagnostic Imaging, Pathology, Cosmetic/Aesthetic & IVF/ART

> Four niches that look unrelated but share one **strategic shape**, so they share a pathway:
> 1. **Highly consolidated** — a handful of corporate-HQ buyers control most of each market
>    ("win the HQ → network-wide rollout").
> 2. **Mandatory or near-mandatory accreditation** is the unavoidable, recurring compliance hook
>    (DIAS / NATA-RCPA ISO 15189 are *required for Medicare*; NSQHS Cosmetic Surgery / RTAC→ACSQHC
>    for cosmetic & ART).
> 3. **A sharp 2024–2027 regulatory shock** in each is forcing net-new compliance/QMS/training spend
>    *right now*.
> 4. **AI = SaMD risk** is acute (image/embryo interpretation are squarely TGA medical-device
>    territory) — so ClinicBoss must stay firmly on the **compliance-evidence/QMS** side.
>
> The common GTM play: **sell the accreditation-evidence / QMS / training layer to corporate HQ,
> timed to the regulatory shock.** This is **Wave 3** (high-value, concentrated, longer cycle).
> These were flagged for follow-up in `06-outliers.md`; this doc is that follow-up.
>

## B. Regulatory drivers (the hooks, by niche)

- **Imaging — DIAS** (Diagnostic Imaging Accreditation Scheme): legally tied to Medicare benefit
  payment — **not accredited = cannot bill Medicare**. 15 standards/4 parts; full accreditation
  4-yearly; accreditors NATA/HDAA/QIP. Underpinned by **RANZCR Standards (2025)**; **ARPANSA RPS C-5**
  + the **2024 diagnostic-imaging-apparatus performance-testing standard**; state radiation
  licensing; **NDRLs** for dose. **MHR share-by-default** from 1 Jul 2026 (radiologist reports).
- **Pathology — NATA/RCPA ISO 15189**: required for Approved Pathology Authority/Laboratory status
  and Medicare billing — **no accreditation = no Medicare**. **NPAAC** category-of-testing standards
  layered on top. **ISO 15189:2022 transition (application document effective Feb 2026)** forces a
  fresh QMS/document-control update wave. **MHR share-by-default** (pathologist reports) 1 Jul 2026.
- **Cosmetic** — **NSQHS Cosmetic Surgery Standards** (launched Dec 2023; 7 standards/101 actions;
  accreditation from early 2025) mandatory for invasive surgery under sedation/GA; **"surgeon" title
  protection** (Sep 2023); **AHPRA cosmetic surgery reforms** (Jul 2023: referral, two consults, BDD
  screening); **AHPRA non-surgical cosmetic guidelines** (eff. 2 Sep 2025: RN experience minimums,
  in-person/video consult each prescribe, mandatory CPD, influencer/under-18 advertising bans);
  **TGA injectables advertising crackdown** (2025: prohibited terms, before/after bans; **98 of 100
  clinic sites found non-compliant**; penalties up to A$1.65m individual / A$16.5m corporate per
  breach; Targeted Compliance Unit active).
- **IVF/ART** — currently **RTAC Code of Practice** (FSANZ, self-regulatory); after the 2024–25
  **Monash IVF embryo incidents**, Health Ministers (Sep 2025) agreed **ACSQHC will independently
  accredit ART against national standards from 2027** (standards project began Apr 2026; in place by
  Jan 2027), with new **minimum qualifications + CPD for embryologists/nurses/counsellors/doctors**
  and a **national donor registry**. State fragmentation accelerating reform (VIC: VARTA abolished,
  Dept of Health regulator from 1 Jan 2025; QLD ART Act 2024 from Sep 2025; WA Bill 2025, regime
  ~mid-2027).

## C. Funding levers

- **Imaging & pathology:** **MBS is dominant** (bulk-billed imaging at 95% of schedule fee; high path
  bulk-billing). 2024–25 Budget: A$162.6m imaging reform; **new annual indexation** for labour-
  intensive pathology groups (P1/P4/P5/P6/P8). Buyers are **large, well-capitalised corporates** →
  enterprise deal economics.
- **Cosmetic:** **private out-of-pocket**, high-margin/discretionary → strong ability to fund
  compliance; regulation is now a non-optional cost of doing business.
- **IVF/ART:** **MBS-subsidised** (items 13200/13201/13218; no federal cycle cap; Safety Net ~A$2,615)
  + ~A$5–6.7k out-of-pocket/cycle + state rebates (NSW/ACT ~A$2,000). Profitable corporatised groups.

## D. Market fit — why Dromaios wins here

Each niche has **entrenched clinical systems** we will *not* fight — RIS/PACS (Karisma/Comrad/
Intelerad/Sectra) and LIS (AUSLAB/Cerner/Delphic) in diagnostics; aesthetic EMRs (Aesthetic Record,
Pabau, Consentz) in cosmetic; ART EMRs (MedITEX, Baby Sentry, eIVF) and electronic-witnessing
(RI Witness) in IVF. The **white space is the compliance/QMS/accreditation-evidence + training
layer** — and notably **no dominant AU compliance/LMS/incident vendor surfaced in cosmetic or IVF**.
The incumbent QMS comparators (Logiqc, Ideagen Q-Pulse, RLDatix) are beatable on a healthcare-
specific, accreditation-mapped, AI-evidence-assisted offering — *especially* during the live
re-accreditation/transition shocks. We integrate with the clinical systems; we own the audit.

## H. Pricing & packaging

- **Enterprise, per-network / flat-unlimited** — these are large corporates; price for network-wide
  QMS + training, multi-year, with implementation/data-migration services (well-capitalised buyers
  tolerate enterprise economics).
- **Independent cosmetic surgical facilities** and smaller IVF/imaging sites: per-site + per-learner/yr.
- **M&A-harmonisation** projects (imaging/pathology) can be sold as a services + platform package.
- High WTP across all four: penalty exposure (cosmetic), Medicare-dependency (imaging/path),
  reputational-catastrophe risk (IVF) all justify premium pricing.

## K. AI plays & guardrails

- **Plays (safe, non-SaMD):** accreditation-**evidence assembler** (DIAS/ISO 15189/NSQHS/RTAC);
  **advertising-compliance scanner** (flags prohibited injectable terms/before-after imagery — a
  natural, high-value AI use given the 98/100 breach rate); QMS document generation; dose-analytics
  vs NDRLs; ISO 15189 transition gap-analysis; MHR upload-assurance checks.
- **Hard guardrails:** **image interpretation (radiology/digital pathology) and embryo
  selection/grading are TGA SaMD** — regulated by intended purpose; ClinicBoss must **never** enter
  diagnostic/selection territory. Note OAIC ran preliminary privacy inquiries into I-MED/Harrison.ai/
  Annalise.ai over training-data — **data residency/onshore + APP compliance are decisive**; genomic
  and whole-slide/embryo data are highly sensitive. Automated-decision disclosure by 10 Dec 2026.

## L. Risks & competitive notes

- **Incumbent QMS** (Logiqc, Ideagen Q-Pulse, RLDatix) are real — beat on healthcare-specific,
  accreditation-mapped, AI-assisted evidence + the live-transition timing.
- **Concentration risk:** few buyers per niche → long, lumpy sales; a lost HQ is a lost market slice.
  Balance with independent surgical/smaller-site self-serve.
- **Clinical-system entrenchment** (RIS/PACS, LIS, ART EMR, RI Witness) — integrate, never replace.
- **IVF reputational sensitivity** — sell trust/safety/evidence, never exploit the scandal; expect
  intense due diligence on our own data security.
- **AI-SaMD line** — discipline required; one diagnostic-sounding feature could pull ClinicBoss into
  TGA device regulation and blow the low-risk thesis.
