# Pathway 05 — Mining / Remote-Site / Occupational Health

> Mine-site medics, FIFO health, occupational-health contractors, pre-employment medicals, drug &
> alcohol testing, fitness-for-work, emergency response. The **highest willingness-to-pay** segment
> (compliance = licence to operate; director liability), but **procurement/contractor-driven** with
> entrenched WHS/contractor-management platforms. **Wave 2** — entered via **safety training**
> (OVA, fatigue, D&A, fitness-for-work), which has high WTP and a short cycle, then ClinicBoss
> occupational-health/compliance modules.

## A. Snapshot

- **>330 operating mines** (end-2024; some sources >350). **Mining workforce ~234,690 (ABS FY2024)**;
  Minerals Council cites ~303,300. WA holds ~47%; record **135,693 on-site FTE**. **FIFO** large but
  no authoritative national total (Pilbara alone ~13,384 mining FIFO, 2023) — *flag: FIFO national
  figure estimated*.
- **Occupational-health provider structure:** dominated by **Sonic HealthPlus** (largest AU occ-
  health provider), plus regional players; fragmented below the top tier.
- **Economic buyer:** **HSE/HSEQ Manager**, **Health & Safety Superintendent**, **Site Senior
  Executive (SSE)** (statutory in QLD coal), corporate procurement (sets the contractor compliance
  bar), and the **occupational-health contractor** itself (buys clinical/testing software).
- **Pipeline tailwind:** AREEA forecasts ~96 projects, ~22,300 jobs, **A$129.5bn investment
  2025–2030** — a growing addressable base.

---

## B. Regulatory drivers

- **State-based mining WHS** (not unified): NSW Resources Regulator (WHS (Mines & Petroleum Sites)
  Act/Reg 2022); **QLD Resources Safety & Health Queensland (RSHQ)**; WA DMIRS (WHS (Mines) Reg 2022).
- **QLD RSHLA Act 2024** — major near-term driver: new provisions commenced **1 Jun 2025**; the
  **critical-controls compliance deadline is 1 Jun 2026**. Note the **safety-critical-role
  competency/certificate transition runs to 1 Jun 2030** (full assessment for new applicants from
  1 Jun 2026), so the *immediate* 2026 demand is critical-controls assurance + induction/SHMS for
  Remote Operating Centre workers, with competency uplift a longer (to-2030) tail — don't oversell
  2026 as a competency cliff.
- **Coal Mine Workers' Health Scheme (QLD)** + NSW Coal Services — mandatory employer-funded periodic
  health assessments (spirometry, chest imaging) for fitness-for-work and CWP/silicosis detection.
- **Dust/respiratory:** NSW respirable coal dust WES 1.5 mg/m³ (2021); QLD Chapter 8A crystalline
  silica (silica control plans) from 1 Sep 2024.
- **Drug & alcohol:** **AS/NZS 4308:2023** (urine), AS/NZS 4760 (oral fluid), AS 3547 (breath); mining
  commonly mandates 0.00 BAC.
- **ISO 45001** OHS management systems widely adopted; fitness-for-work codes (Safe Work Australia);
  emergency-response standards; **RII training-package** competencies (recurring training spend).

## C. Funding levers

Mining **capex/opex safety budgets** are large and non-discretionary (licence to operate);
**employer-funded** statutory health assessments (CMWHS); **WorkCover**; **contractor firms pass
compliance costs** to win site access. This is the segment where buyers **expect** to pay well for
safety/compliance — the opposite of the NDIS margin squeeze.

WHS/EHS software (**HSI Donesafe, myosh, WHS Monitor, Cority, INX**) and contractor-management/site-
access platforms (**Avetta, Cm3, Rapid Global, Damstra, Pegasus**) are entrenched and create a

generic WHS vendors don't specialise in, and (2) a **fitness-for-work + health-surveillance
compliance module (V-MINE)** for the **occupational-health contractors** (e.g. the Sonic HealthPlus
long tail of regional providers) who need clinical-compliance tooling, not just an EHS platform.

## K. AI plays & guardrails

- **Plays:** AI **compliance-evidence generation** (health-surveillance/competency records),
  **fatigue analytics** (aggregate, not individualised clinical judgement), D&A program reporting.
- **Guardrails:** **fitness-for-work decisions must remain human-made and defensible** (Fair Work/
  tribunal exposure) — AI assists, never decides; **biometric fatigue monitoring** raises privacy/
  surveillance concerns (consent, proportionality); health-surveillance data is sensitive (APP,
  onshore); non-SaMD. **Automated-decision-making disclosure (OAIC, from 10 Dec 2026) bites
  specifically where AI materially informs a decision that significantly affects a person's rights/
  interests — e.g. fitness-for-work** — so attach the disclosure to those high-impact features, not as
  a blanket label on every AI play.

## L. Risks & competitive notes

- **Entrenched WHS/EHS + contractor platforms** (Donesafe, myosh, Avetta, Cm3) — don't compete on
  generic WHS/contractor management; win healthcare-grade training + clinical fitness-for-work
  compliance; interoperate.
- **State fragmentation** (NSW/QLD/WA regimes differ) — crosswalks must be state-specific; start with
  QLD (RSHLA deadline) and WA (largest workforce).
- **Sonic HealthPlus dominance** in occ-health delivery — target the regional/contractor long tail,
  not the giant.
- **Defensibility of AI-assisted fitness-for-work** — keep humans in the loop; document rationale.
