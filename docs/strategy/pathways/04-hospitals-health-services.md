# Pathway 04 — Hospitals & Health Services (incl. day surgeries)

> Public hospitals / LHDs / HHSs and private hospitals / day procedure services. All run on the
> **NSQHS Standards (2nd ed., 8 standards)** with **short-notice accreditation since 1 Jul 2023**.
> This is the **hardest** segment to enter (enterprise/tender cycles, entrenched **RLDatix**
> incumbency) — so it's **Wave 3**, entered via two beachheads: **OVA training** (the crowbar) and
> **day surgeries** (smaller, sharp short-notice-audit pain, same NSQHS engine). Day surgeries are
> folded in here, not in "outliers," because they share the NSQHS pathway.
>
> Cross-references: `../clinicboss-module-catalogue.md` (V-HOSP, V-OVA) and
> `../dromaiosed-course-catalogue.md` (F01, S06, F03, F04).

## A. Snapshot

- **~700 public hospitals** (2022–23); **633 declared private hospitals** (Sep 2025);
  **>350 day hospitals / day procedure services** (peak-body est.) — ~60% of acute surgical
  procedures now in day hospitals (up to ~90% in ophthalmology). *Flag: comprehensive private-
  hospital structural data is stale — last full ABS collection 2016–17.*
- **Structure:** state systems via **LHDs** (NSW: 15 + 2 specialty networks) / **HHSs** (QLD); major
  private groups **Ramsay** (largest), **Healthscope** (in receivership/sale 2025), **St Vincent's**,
  **Healthe Care**.
- **Economic buyer:** **Director of Clinical Governance / Quality & Safety Manager**, **DON**,
  **Executive/CEO**, **Credentialing/Medical Advisory Committee**. Public ICT ≥A$150k routes through
  **statewide procurement** (HealthShare NSW, eHealth NSW). Private routes via group head offices.

---

## B. Regulatory drivers

- **NSQHS 2nd ed.** — 8 standards (Clinical Governance; Partnering with Consumers; Preventing &
  Controlling Infections; Medication Safety; Comprehensive Care; Communicating for Safety; Blood
  Management; Recognising & Responding to Acute Deterioration); 148 actions. Mandatory for all
  public/private hospitals, **day hospitals**, and most public dental.
- **Short-notice accreditation mandatory since 1 Jul 2023** → continuous, "always audit-ready"
  evidence demand (the core spend driver). Accrediting agencies: **ACHS** (largest), **QIP**, others.
- **Credentialing & scope of practice** (ACSQHC standard; committee-governed); open disclosure;
  mandatory training/competency; IPC/hand hygiene; National Standards for Mental Health.
- **NSQHS 3rd ed.** in development (release ~2028, assessment ~2030) — medium-term, not immediate.

## C. Funding levers

- **Activity-Based Funding** (IHACPA; **NEP 2026–27 = A$7,418/NWAU**, released Mar 2026, up from
  $7,258 in 2025–26); **private health insurance**
  (12.69M / 45.6% with hospital cover, Dec 2025); DVA; WorkCover/CTP. Private-hospital margin stress
  (Healthscope receivership; proposed fixed pricing) → **cost discipline on overhead software**.

**WTP:** high for incident/GRC and credentialing (event volumes are huge — St Vincent's: ~24,000
staff users, >36,000 events in six months), but **enterprise, tender-gated, six-/seven-figure**.

## D. Market fit — why Dromaios wins here

The incumbent is **RLDatix** (RiskMan incident/risk, PROMPT policy, cGov credentialing) — dominant
and consolidated. Displacing it head-on is not the move for a new entrant. The realistic fit:
1. **OVA training** — a genuine, surging, under-served need (hospital assaults up ~44–60%
   2015→2018; violence/assault workers'-comp claims +56%) where we lead with the DromaiosEd flagship,
   not against RLDatix.
2. **Day surgeries** — smaller facilities feeling the **short-notice NSQHS** pain acutely, with
   lighter incumbent penetration and faster (non-statewide) procurement — the ideal NSQHS beachhead
   for ClinicBoss **V-HOSP**.

## E. Entry wedge

**Education-led (OVA crowbar) → day-surgery accreditation pack.** Lead with CPD-accredited,
healthcare-specific **OVA/de-escalation (F01)** + post-incident support (differentiated from free
state OVA training), landing in EDs, mental health units, and aged-care-adjacent wards. Separately,
sell **ClinicBoss V-HOSP (NSQHS readiness)** into **day surgeries** as the low-friction software
beachhead, then expand toward private groups and LHDs.

crosswalk, all 8 standards**) + C8 Dashboard + **V-HOSP** (hand-hygiene/IPC audit, short-notice-

than a rip-and-replace.

credentialing/scope-of-practice committee workflow; OVA analytics; open-disclosure workflow.

## H. Pricing & packaging

- **Day surgeries:** ClinicBoss **per-site/year** (or flat-unlimited for small networks) + DromaiosEd
  per-learner/yr; implementation ~$3–10k (waive for 1–2 lighthouse day hospitals).
- **OVA training:** per-learner/yr eLearning + **instructor-led per-cohort/day** (network-delivered,
  Tier 3) — sellable into LHDs/private groups *without* a software deal.
- **Enterprise (private groups/LHDs):** quote, six-/seven-figure; multi-year; module-bundled.
  Pursue last and via reference customers.

## I. Channel & sales motion

- **Day Hospitals Australia** conference + **APHA** (private hospitals) — the day-surgery channel.
- **OVA**: direct to safety/quality units + WHS leads; state OVA-reduction programs as a tailwind.
- **Public**: statewide procurement (HealthShare NSW, eHealth NSW), panels/standing offers — slow,
  pursue once referenceable.
- **Private**: group head offices (Ramsay, St Vincent's, Healthe Care).
- **Credibility**: **HIC** / Australian Healthcare Week for the software story; **ACSQHC** alignment
  language throughout. Long cycles (6–18+ months for enterprise).

## J. Strategy to move through the stream

1. **OVA beachhead (M0–9):** sell CPD-accredited OVA eLearning + instructor-led into EDs/MH units and
   private groups — revenue and references **without** needing a software win first.
2. **Day-surgery software land (M6–15):** ClinicBoss V-HOSP NSQHS-readiness into day hospitals via
   Day Hospitals Australia; win 1–2 lighthouse facilities through a short-notice-audit cycle.
3. **Private-group expansion (M12–24):** bundle OVA + accreditation evidence + credentialing into
   private groups; interoperate with incumbent RiskMan/PROMPT.
4. **LHD/public (M18–30):** pursue statewide procurement once referenceable; lead with OVA + evidence
   layer, not incident-system replacement.
5. **Metrics:** OVA seats + instructor-led cohorts; day-surgery audit pass/readiness score;
   enterprise pipeline through procurement gates.

## K. AI plays & guardrails

- **Plays:** AI **accreditation-evidence assembler** (NSQHS), **incident triage assist** (category/
  severity/reportability — human confirms), **OVA hotspot analytics**, credentialing automation,
  policy generation. Hospital ambient-scribe trials exist (e.g. Gold Coast Health) — **integrate**,
  don't build.
- **Guardrails:** non-SaMD (TGA regulates clinical AI as SaMD; Feb 2026 guidance removed the
  exemption path for clinically-influential AI); onshore data residency; APP-compliant; clinician-in-
  the-loop; automated-decision disclosure by 10 Dec 2026.

## L. Risks & competitive notes

- **RLDatix incumbency** across incident/policy/credentialing — do not rip-and-replace; win OVA +
  accreditation-evidence + day surgeries; interoperate.
- **Long, tender-gated cycles** — fund the segment from OVA training and day-surgery wins; don't
  burn cash chasing LHD tenders early.
- **Stale private-hospital data** — size pipeline via Day Hospitals Australia/APHA, not ABS.
- **Free state OVA training** — differentiate on healthcare-specific, competency-assessed, CPD-
  accredited, blended + post-incident-support depth.
