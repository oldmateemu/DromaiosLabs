# Pathway 03 — Allied Health

> Physiotherapy, occupational therapy, podiatry, psychology, dietetics, speech pathology, exercise
> physiology, osteopathy, chiropractic, etc. — both AHPRA-registered and self-regulated professions.
> A **bottom-up, self-serve** market: mostly sole traders/small practices, consolidating fast. The
> wedge here is **software/CPD-led self-serve**, not enterprise selling. This is a **Wave 1** entry
> because it overlaps NDIS/aged-care revenue and rides the ambient-AI-scribe wave.
>
> Cross-references: `../clinicboss-module-catalogue.md` (V-AH) and
> `../dromaiosed-course-catalogue.md` (S13, S14, F01, U-stack).

---

## A. Snapshot

- **~959,838 total AHPRA-registered practitioners** (2024–25, all professions; +4.3% YoY) — allied
  health is a large slice. Indicative profession counts: psychologists ~36,900 (2024); OTs ~29,473
  (Mar 2023, now likely >32k); physios ~33,000 (APA membership, not the register — flag);
  podiatrists/others per Board statistics. **Self-regulated**: dietitians (~8,000 DA members),
  speech pathologists (~7,000), exercise physiologists (ESSA).
- **Highly fragmented** (sole traders/small practices) but **consolidating fast** — e.g. **Healthia**
  (1,100+ clinicians, 320+ clinics); PE clinic roll-ups rising; **mandatory ACCC merger regime from
  1 Jan 2026** will scrutinise serial acquisitions.
- **Economic buyer:** **practice owner/principal** + **practice manager** (bottom-up). In roll-up
  groups, a head-office operations/clinical-governance function becomes an enterprise buyer.

---

## B. Regulatory drivers

- **AHPRA registration + CPD** for the 15 registered professions (profession-specific hours;
  reflective practice). **Self-regulated** professions rely on association credentials (APD via
  Dietitians Australia; SPA; NASRHP).
- **CPD tracking + clinical-supervision logs** (provisional psychologists, new grads, students) are
  the core *native* compliance need.
- **Downstream payer requirements drive most compliance spend:** NDIS provider registration & worker
  screening, Aged Care Quality Standards (Support at Home), Medicare eligibility/referral rules, and
  **QIC Health & Community Services Standards** for community/allied orgs.

---

## C. Funding levers

- **Medicare/MBS:** from **1 Jul 2025** the **GP Chronic Condition Management Plan (GPCCMP)**
  replaced GPMP+TCA; up to **5 allied-health services/yr** (10 for Aboriginal/Torres Strait Islander
  patients); referral by letter.
- **NDIS** is a dominant allied-health revenue source but **therapy caps held tight** (physio
  $193.99/hr largely unchanged since
  2019; travel now 50%) → **margin squeeze** (34% of allied-health providers reported a loss in
  2022–23). **Support at Home** (1 Nov 2025) adds a **Restorative Care Pathway (up to 16 weeks,
  ~$6,000/episode, up to 2 episodes)** with multidisciplinary allied-health input, and reclassifies
  showering as clinical care — an allied-health opportunity.
- Also: private-health "extras", WorkCover/CTP, DVA.

**WTP:** price-sensitive (squeezed margins) → **low monthly per-seat**, compliance/LMS as an
add-on, not standalone enterprise spend. Volume + education attach is the play.

---

## D. Market fit — why Dromaios wins here

The practice-management layer is crowded and cheap (**Cliniko, Zanda Health (ex-Power Diary),
Halaxy, Nookal, Coreplus, Splose, PracSuite**) and ambient AI scribes (**Heidi, Lyrebird**) are the
breakout feature. We **don't** build another PMS. The fit is a **light compliance + CPD + supervision
layer** that (a) the registered professions need anyway and (b) becomes essential the moment a
practice does NDIS/aged-care work — i.e. a bridge that connects allied-health practices to Pathway 02
obligations. Education (CPD-accredited) is the cheap, recurring door.

---

## E. Entry wedge

**Software/CPD-led self-serve.** Lead with a **low-cost CPD logbook + reflective-practice course
(S13) + clinical-supervision tooling (S14/V-AH)** — frictionless self-signup, viral within
professions. Attach the **mandatory stack + OVA (F01)** for practices doing NDIS/aged-care/home-care
work, then the relevant ClinicBoss crosswalks from Pathway 02 when they need provider registration.

---

## F. ClinicBoss rollout & required functions

**Phase 1 (land, self-serve):** **V-AH** — CPD logbook (maps to each profession's CPD standard),
**clinical-supervision logs** (supervisor + supervisee, hours, competencies), simple policy/consent
templates. Plus C3 Training register.
**Phase 2 (expand):** C2 Policy + C4 readiness (pull in **NDIS Practice Standards / Aged Care
Standards / QIC** crosswalks from Pathway 02 for practices doing funded work) + C1 incidents (NDIS
reportable incidents) + C5 credentialing (AHPRA registration, worker screening).
**Phase 3 (groups):** flat-unlimited for roll-up groups (Healthia-type) standardising tooling across
clinics; integrate with Cliniko/Halaxy/Nookal via API.

**Required net-new functions:** profession-specific CPD logbooks; supervision-log workflow;
PMS integrations (Cliniko/Halaxy/Nookal); reuse of Pathway 02 crosswalks.

## G. DromaiosEd modules required

Tier 0 universal stack (lighter touch for small practices). Tier 1: **F01 OVA** (lone/community

## H. Pricing & packaging

- **Per-practitioner/month**, low (compete near Cliniko/Zanda price points for the compliance/CPD
  add-on, not the PMS). Free CPD-logbook tier → paid compliance/supervision tiers (freemium funnel).
- **Per-learner/yr** for the education catalogue; **CPD-accredited** flagships at premium.
- **Flat-unlimited/yr** for groups. Keep it cheap — margins are thin; win on volume + attach.

---

## I. Channel & sales motion

- **Bottom-up self-serve** (free trials, low price, viral within professions) — the dominant motion.
- **Peak bodies as endorsement/discount channels:** **AHPA** (umbrella ~30 professions), **APA**
  (physio; FOCUS26 conference), OT Australia, Dietitians Australia, SPA, ESSA — replicate the NDS
  member-discount model.
- **Roll-up groups** (Healthia/Advent) as a top-down enterprise opportunity (standardised tooling).
- **Adjacency to Pathway 02:** every NDIS/aged-care allied-health practice is a warm lead for the
  full compliance engine.

---

## J. Strategy to move through the stream

1. **Free CPD hook (M0–4):** launch the CPD logbook + reflective-practice course free/cheap;
   self-serve signup; seed via APA/AHPA channels. Build a user base across professions.
2. **Supervision + compliance upsell (M3–9):** convert to paid V-AH (supervision logs) and the
   mandatory + OVA stack, especially for NDIS/aged-care practices.
3. **Funded-work bridge (M6–15):** for practices doing NDIS/Support-at-Home, attach the Pathway 02
   crosswalks (registration/audit readiness) — cross-sell into the care engine.
4. **Group rollout (M12+):** flat-unlimited deals to consolidating allied-health groups; PMS
   integrations as the stickiness lever.
5. **Metrics:** free→paid conversion; supervision-log MAU; cross-sell rate into Pathway 02 packs.

---

## K. AI plays & guardrails

- **Plays:** **integrate** ambient scribes (Heidi/Lyrebird explicitly target allied health/
  psychology) rather than build — pair with our compliance record for the documentation story; AI
  CPD-reflection prompts and supervision-note structuring (human-owned); evidence assembler for
  NDIS/aged-care audits.
- **Guardrails:** non-SaMD; onshore; APP-compliant; **scribe use needs client consent + clinician
  oversight**; generative tools that *influence care* risk SaMD scope — stay on the documentation/
  compliance side; automated-decision disclosure by 10 Dec 2026.

---

## L. Risks & competitive notes

- **Crowded, cheap PMS market** — do **not** compete on PMS; be the compliance/CPD/supervision layer
  and integrate.
- **Thin margins** (tight NDIS therapy caps) — price low; rely on volume + education attach + group deals.
- **Self-regulated professions** have softer mandates — lead with CPD value + funded-work
  obligations, not fear.
- **ACCC merger regime (1 Jan 2026)** may slow roll-ups — keep both bottom-up and group motions live.
