# Rung-2 Offer — "Safer Teams Program" (the embedding program)

The second rung in the offer ladder (`CUSTOMER_ACQUISITION_STRATEGY.md` §3). Where the rung-1
workshop (`workshop-package-rung1.md`) is a one-off that changes the room for a day, this is a
quarter-long program that changes how a team *works* — and it's the first piece of **recurring
revenue**. Its job: turn happy workshop clients into multi-session, multi-site, repeatable accounts,
and build the deep relationship that earns a ClinicBoss design-partner conversation later.

## The offer in one line
A 12-week program that sets a shared safety-and-documentation standard across your team or sites,
practises it, and embeds it — so "good" becomes repeatable instead of depending on who's working.

## Why it exists (the gap rung 1 leaves)
A great workshop teaches. Embedding is what makes it stick. Most training fades within a month
because nothing reinforces it on a busy floor. The Safer Teams Program closes that embedding gap
with a set standard, spaced reinforcement, and named ownership over a quarter.

## Who it's for
- Care providers who ran a rung-1 workshop and want it to *last*.
- Multi-site or multi-shift operators who need consistency across teams, not just one good session.
- Leaders who keep solving the same safety/documentation problems and want them solved once, properly.

## The outcome (what the team can do and sustain by the end)
- Operate to one **shared, written standard** for the first 30 seconds and for documentation.
- Run their **own** rehearsals and refreshers without external help.
- Hold **named ownership** for each key safety/compliance area.
- Onboard new starters **into the standard**, so it survives turnover.
- Show a **consistent evidence trail** across shifts and sites.

---

## Structure (12 weeks)

| Phase | Weeks | What happens |
|-------|-------|--------------|
| **Set** | 1–2 | Baseline (team self-assessment + a look at real, de-identified records). Agree the shared standard for escalation response and documentation. |
| **Practise** | 3–8 | Two facilitated sessions + structured between-session practice. Build the Say/Do/Call playbook and the documentation shape into reflexes. Name owners. |
| **Embed** | 9–11 | Light-touch reinforcement: short check-ins, a "train the team to run its own rehearsal" session, onboarding-into-the-standard. |
| **Lock in** | 12 | Review against the baseline (capability and consistency, described qualitatively), agree what the team will sustain, and hand over the materials so it runs without us. |

**Delivery:** blended — a small number of facilitated sessions (on-site or virtual) plus structured
self-run practice between them. Designed to fit around rosters, not fight them.

**Cohort options:**
- **Single team** — one intact team through all 12 weeks.
- **Multi-site** — parallel cohorts with a shared standard, so sites converge rather than drift.

---

## What's included
- Facilitated sessions across the quarter (number set by cohort size/scope).
- The shared-standard templates (Say/Do/Call playbook + documentation shape + ownership map).
- Between-session practice structures the team runs themselves.
- A baseline and end-of-program review (qualitative — capability and consistency, not outcome claims).
- Take-home materials so the standard is sustained after the program ends.

## Pricing posture
Keep pricing as a fixed, transparent **program fee** (not hourly), billed per cohort. This is the
recurring-revenue rung, so make renewal natural:
- **Single-team program** — flat quarterly fee.
- **Multi-site** — per-cohort fee with a bundle rate across sites.
- **Annual** — bridge to rung 3: the program plus ongoing quarterly refreshers and practical tools
  (HIL/Skool resources) as a yearly arrangement.

(Set actual numbers from cost/market. Per the guardrail, keep pricing in proposals, not public copy.)

Anchor on value honestly: consistency that survives turnover and busy weeks — without promising a
specific reduction in incidents or audit findings.

---

## How it ladders
- **From rung 1:** offer the program at the close of a workshop, or 6–8 weeks after, when the team
  has felt the value but also felt the fade. "The workshop set it; the program makes it stick."
- **To rung 3:** at week 12, offer the annual arrangement (refreshers + tools) so the standard is
  maintained and you become the team's ongoing safety/documentation partner.
- **To ClinicBoss (earned only):** a team that has worked with you for a quarter and has a clear
  operational-software pain is exactly who you open a *problem-level* ClinicBoss design-partner
  conversation with — never as part of this program's pitch, and never with feature claims.

## Post-program (turn delivery into renewal + pipeline)
- Run the qualitative end-of-program review *with* the leader — it's the renewal conversation.
- Offer the rung-3 annual arrangement before the program closes.
- Request a careful testimonial/case note (`testimonial-and-case-note-template.md`) — no outcome
  numbers unless measured and approved.
- For the right accounts, open the earned, problem-level ClinicBoss conversation.
- Log the account in the cockpit sales function: program delivered → renewal stage → expansion.
