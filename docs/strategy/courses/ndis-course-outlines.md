# DromaiosEd — NDIS Course Outlines (Launch Catalogue v1)

> The concrete content spec for the **NDIS education catalogue** referenced in
> `../launch-plans/90-day-ndis-launch.md` (Phase 1, Workstream A) and the
> `../dromaiosed-course-catalogue.md`. These are the build-ready outlines an author (AI-drafted,
> clinician-validated) turns into eLearning modules.
>
> **Scope & posture.** Education only — **non-SaMD**, AU-data-resident, clinician-reviewed before
> publish. These courses **complement** (never duplicate) the free, mandatory **NDIS Worker
> Orientation Module "Quality, Safety and You."** Where a topic is high-risk (medication, manual
> handling, restrictive practices), the eLearning is **knowledge + awareness**; the **competency
> sign-off** is captured separately in ClinicBoss C3 (practical assessment by a qualified assessor).
>
> **Standards anchors** (state in each course's "maps to"): NDIS Practice Standards & Quality
> Indicators, NDIS Code of Conduct, NDIS (Incident Management and Reportable Incidents) Rules,
> NDIS (Restrictive Practices and Behaviour Support) Rules, the Worker Capability Framework. **Verify
> the current version of each instrument before publish** (regulation changes around the 1 Jul 2026
> mandatory-registration reforms).

## Standard module structure (applied to every course)

1. **Title, audience, duration, standards mapping** (the "maps to" line).
2. **Learning outcomes** (3–6, observable/assessable verbs).
3. **Why this matters** (real disability-sector context; the obligation + the person-centred "why").
4. **Core content** (sectioned; plain English; scenarios from disability settings — SIL, SDA,
   community, in-home).
5. **Knowledge check** (≥ 8–10 items; scenario-based where possible; 80% pass).
6. **Reflective / application task** (links learning to the worker's own practice).
7. **Competency sign-off pointer** (where practical assessment is required — handed to ClinicBoss C3).
8. **Summary + resources** (links to the relevant NDIS Commission resource; version + review date).

## N-OVA — Occupational Violence & Aggression / De-escalation (Disability) ⭐

- **Audience:** all frontline disability workers, team leaders, coordinators.
- **Maps to:** NDIS Practice Standards (incident management; risk management); WHS duties;
  Worker Capability Framework (responding to behaviours of concern). **Differentiator vs free/generic
  OVA training:** disability-specific, competency-assessable, CPD-accredited, multi-state, blended.
- **Learning outcomes:** by the end, the worker can —
  1. Recognise early warning signs and triggers of escalation in disability-support contexts.
  2. Apply non-physical **de-escalation** techniques appropriate to the person and setting.
  3. Distinguish **behaviour of concern** (support need) from intentional aggression, and respond
     within a person-centred, trauma-informed, least-restrictive frame.
  4. Take correct **post-incident** steps: immediate safety, reporting, debrief, support.
  5. Identify when an incident is **reportable** (links to N-RIM) and when a restrictive practice
     was used (links to N-RP).
- **Core sections:** the disability context of OVA (prevalence, duty of care, worker right to safety);
  understanding behaviour as communication; environmental & relational triggers; the de-escalation
  toolkit (verbal, non-verbal, space, choice, co-regulation); cultural & communication-access
  considerations (non-speaking participants, AAC); when to disengage and seek help; post-incident
  response & worker wellbeing/trauma support.
- **Assessment:** branching scenarios (choose the de-escalation response) + reflective task on a
  real/at-risk situation. **Optional practical sign-off** for high-risk roles (ClinicBoss C3).
- **Notes:** keep strictly **non-restrictive** — this course teaches de-escalation, *not* restraint;
  restraint/restrictive practices are N-RP and are tightly regulated.

## N-REG — NDIS Registration & Practice Standards Readiness ⭐

- **Audience:** provider owners/MDs, quality & compliance managers, practice managers.
- **Maps to:** NDIS Practice Standards (all applicable modules); the registration process
  (verification vs certification); the 1 Jul 2026 mandatory-registration reforms.
- **Learning outcomes:**
  1. Explain the registration pathways — **verification** (desktop) vs **certification** (on-site)
     — and which applies to the provider's registration groups (esp. SIL/SDA/behaviour support).
  2. Map the provider's services to the applicable **Practice Standards & Quality Indicators**.
  3. Identify the **evidence** an auditor expects and where the provider's gaps are.
  4. Prepare for and pass an audit; understand surveillance and ongoing obligations.
  5. Understand the **mandatory-registration changes** and what they mean for SIL/platform providers.
- **Core sections:** the Commission & the regulatory model; registration groups & risk; the audit
  journey (self-assessment → stage 1 → stage 2 → outcome); evidence types (policies, records,
  worker screening, training, incident data); common non-conformities and how to avoid them; the
  reform timeline. **Ties directly to ClinicBoss C4** (the Practice Standards crosswalk does the
  evidence-mapping the course teaches).
- **Assessment:** knowledge check + a **readiness self-audit** the learner completes for their org
  (exportable; becomes a ClinicBoss action list).

## N-RP — Restrictive Practices & Positive Behaviour Support ⭐

- **Audience:** workers in services using/at risk of regulated restrictive practices; behaviour-support
  & clinical leads; managers.
- **Maps to:** NDIS (Restrictive Practices and Behaviour Support) Rules; Practice Standards (behaviour
  support module); **state/territory authorisation frameworks** (content localised per state).
- **Learning outcomes:**
  1. Define the **five regulated restrictive practices** and recognise each in practice.
  2. Explain the **authorisation, consent and reporting** requirements (state-specific) and the role
     of a **behaviour support plan**.
  3. Apply **least-restrictive, person-centred** principles and PBS fundamentals.
  4. Correctly **record and report** use of a regulated restrictive practice (links to N-RIM).
  5. Recognise **unauthorised** restrictive practice as a reportable incident.
- **Core sections:** what counts as a restrictive practice; the harm and the rights frame; PBS basics
  (function of behaviour, proactive strategies); authorisation & consent by state; the behaviour
  support plan; recording/reporting; reducing & eliminating restrictive practices over time.
- **Assessment:** knowledge check (scenario-based identification + reporting steps). **Competency
  sign-off in ClinicBoss** for workers implementing behaviour support plans.
- **Notes:** **localise authorisation content per state/territory** — this is the most common audit
  failure point and a genuine differentiator if done well.
- **Phasing (resolves the 90-day scope question):** **Phase 1 ships a generic multi-state template**
  covering the five regulated practices, PBS principles, and general authorisation/consent/reporting
  concepts — authored by AI, clinician-reviewed, and **explicitly labelled "general principles; confirm
  your state's authorisation requirements."** **Phase 2 adds the per-state/territory authorisation
  deep-dives**, each requiring a **state-localisation expert / legal review** before publish (budget a
  fixed-scope reviewer per priority state — e.g. the founder's home state + the two largest markets
  first). Do **not** publish state-specific authorisation rules as authoritative until that review is
  complete. This decision is mirrored in the launch-priority note (above) and the 90-day plan.

## N-CODE — NDIS Code of Conduct & Worker Obligations

- **Maps to:** NDIS Code of Conduct; Worker Capability Framework.
- **Outcomes:** apply the Code's elements (rights, privacy, integrity, safety, acting on concerns,
  preventing violence/exploitation/neglect/abuse) to day-to-day decisions; know how/when to raise
  concerns; understand consequences of breaches. **Scenario-led**, short, foundational.

## N-RIM — Reportable Incidents & Incident Management (NDIS)

> Named for the NDIS **(Incident Management and Reportable Incidents) Rules** — deliberately **not**
> "SIRS," which is the *aged-care* Serious Incident Response Scheme. The ClinicBoss **V-SIRS** pack
> spans both regimes at platform level, but this NDIS course uses NDIS regulator terminology to avoid
> conflating aged-care language with NDIS audit evidence.

- **Maps to:** NDIS (Incident Management and Reportable Incidents) Rules; Practice Standards (incident
  management).
- **Outcomes:** identify what is a **reportable incident**; apply the **24-hour / 5-day** notification
  timeframes; follow the provider's incident-management system; understand worker role in
  recording/escalating; link to restrictive-practice and abuse/neglect reporting. **Ties to ClinicBoss
  V-SIRS** (capture + clocks). Scenario quiz on "is this reportable, and by when?"

## N-SAFE — Safeguarding, Abuse & Neglect Awareness

- **Outcomes:** recognise signs/types of abuse, neglect and exploitation of people with disability;
  understand mandatory responses and reporting; supporting a person to be safe; the worker's
  safeguarding duty. Trauma-informed; links to N-RIM and N-CODE.

## N-MAN — Safe Manual Handling (Disability)

- **Outcomes:** apply safe manual-handling and assisted-transfer principles in disability settings;
  recognise risks; use equipment correctly; person-centred handling (dignity, consent, choice).
  **Knowledge module + required practical competency sign-off** (assessor-led, recorded in ClinicBoss).

## N-IPC — Infection Prevention & Control

- **Outcomes:** standard & transmission-based precautions; hand hygiene; PPE; in-home/community
  considerations; outbreak basics. Foundational, short.

## N-MED — Medication Support & Safety

- **Outcomes:** the worker's role and limits in **medication support** (assist vs administer);
  the rights of medication; recognising/reporting errors and adverse effects; consent & dignity.
  **Knowledge + practical competency sign-off**; flag scope-of-role boundaries clearly.

## N-PRIV — Privacy, Dignity & Information Handling

- **Outcomes:** apply privacy & confidentiality (Privacy Act/APPs context) in support work; dignity
  and respect; handling personal information and records; consent; social-media boundaries.

## N-CULT — Cultural Safety & Inclusive Support

- **Outcomes:** culturally safe, inclusive, anti-discriminatory support; working with Aboriginal &
  Torres Strait Islander participants and CALD communities; intersectionality with disability;
  reflective practice. (Co-design with First Nations clinicians where used for AMS — see Pathway 01.)

## N-PCA — Person-Centred & Rights-Based Support

- **Outcomes:** apply choice & control, dignity of risk, supported decision-making, and a rights-based
  approach; goal-focused support aligned to the participant's plan. Foundational orientation course.

## Packaging for sale (ties to the launch plan)

- **"NDIS Worker Compliance Pack"** — the mandatory stack + N-CODE + N-RIM + N-SAFE, per learner/yr
  (the volume product for the long tail).

## Build & quality checklist (per course, before publish)

- [ ] "Maps to" line cites the **current version** of each NDIS instrument (re-verify around reforms).
- [ ] **Clinician review** complete for any clinical/safety content (N-OVA, N-RP, N-MED, N-MAN, N-RIM).
- [ ] Plain English + accessibility (captions, alt text, screen-reader friendly, Easy Read where apt).
- [ ] Scenarios are disability-sector-authentic (SIL/SDA/community/in-home), not generic.
- [ ] Assessment ≥ 80% pass; competency-sign-off items flagged to **ClinicBoss C3** where practical.
- [ ] Does **not** duplicate the free Worker Orientation Module — complements/extends it.
- [ ] **Non-SaMD**, AU-data-resident; learner data handled per APPs; no clinical decision-making.
- [ ] Version + review date stamped; owner assigned; next review scheduled in the compliance calendar.
- [ ] **CPD accreditation** pathway initiated for N-OVA / N-REG / N-RP (the moat).
