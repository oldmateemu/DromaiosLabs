## 0. The one-sentence thesis

> **Nobody in Australia owns the intersection of "compliance + training + operations as one evidence-linked system," and nobody credibly owns "evidence-led" in safety/de-escalation. Those two open lanes — fused — are where Dromaios can become #1.**

Every competitor is structurally siloed: practice-management software (Cliniko, Splose) does *operations*; LMS players (Ausmed, Altura) do *training*; a new "compliance orchestration" category (Willow, Complynce) exists **only because the first two don't talk to each other**. Dromaios Labs' four-stream model (DromaiosEd + ClinicBoss + HIL/Skool + Medtech) is the rare structure that can unify them — but only if executed as *one connected proof*, not four separate products.

### The 9 most exploitable weaknesses (ranked)

1. **Compliance/accreditation is bolted-on, never built-in.** RACGP/QPA evidence lives in standalone tools; NDIS/aged-care audit-readiness lives in separate care-management apps. **No mainstream allied/clinic PMS embeds continuous compliance into daily workflow.** *(This is ClinicBoss's core thesis — the single biggest open lane.)*
2. **Opaque / surprise pricing** — Halaxy credits balloon 50–100%; Splose in-app add-ons; SMS fees everywhere. Clinics can't predict their bill.
3. **Data-portability failure = audit & lock-in risk** — Helix and MediRecords users can't extract full records. "You own your data, one-click audit-ready export" is a sharp wedge.
4. **Support quality is the #1 recurring complaint** across Zanda, Helix, Zedmed, Heidi, Lyrebird. Human, AU-timezone support is cheap to promise and visibly differentiating.
5. **AI-scribe reliability/trust crisis** (Heidi 3.3 Trustpilot). A *reliable, compliance-grade* scribe is now a wedge — reliability, not novelty, is the bar.
6. **GP incumbents stuck on legacy on-premise** (Bp ~60% share, cloud only from Jul 2025). Migration anxiety + security pressure = switching window.
7. **Per-seat pricing punishes growth** (Nookal +118%, Power Diary +66% on 2nd seat). Coreplus's flat pricing is the only counter and it under-markets it.
8. **Weak finance integration** (Cliniko one-way Xero, no recurring invoices; Halaxy reconciliation errors).
9. **Multi-funder claiming is uneven** — no vendor cleanly does Medicare + DVA + NDIS + aged care in one place.

### The regulatory drivers that *force* training spend

- **Aged Care:** Strengthened Standard 2 (Human Resource Management) **Action 2.9.6** requires *regular competency-based training* (incl. SIRS, Code of Conduct, Quality Standards). Aged Care **Code of Conduct** = 8 mandated behaviours. **SIRS** Priority 1 reporting within 24 hrs requires trained staff. Worker-screening reforms from mid-2026. [ACQSC HR management](https://www.agedcarequality.gov.au/strengthened-quality-standards/organisation/human-resource-management), [ACQSC Code](https://www.agedcarequality.gov.au/workers/code-conduct-aged-care/what-code-conduct-aged-care)
- **NDIS:** mandatory **Worker Orientation Module**; Practice Standards + Behaviour Support & Restrictive Practices Rules require trained PBS practitioners and safe-use-of-restrictive-practice training, refreshed when plans change. [NDIS Commission rules](https://www.ndiscommission.gov.au/rules-and-standards/behaviour-support-and-restrictive-practices/rules-behaviour-support-and)

### The biggest open lane: **"evidence-led" is genuinely unclaimed**

Multiple independent systematic reviews converge: the evidence base for de-escalation training is **inconsistent and weak**. A 2015 review found *no model demonstrated effectiveness in a sufficiently rigorous evaluation*; the NIHR EDITION trial and RAND RR3148 confirm limited evidence; in Australia, ISCRR found *"limited evidence available to confirm the success"* of occupational-violence-prevention efforts. Yet every incumbent (CPI, MAYBO, Team Teach, Resolution Education) sells proprietary frameworks **without robust published efficacy data**.

- [BMC Psychiatry review](https://bmcpsychiatry.biomedcentral.com/articles/10.1186/s12888-023-04714-y) · [EDITION (PMC)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11017147/) · [RAND RR3148](https://www.rand.org/content/dam/rand/pubs/research_reports/RR3100/RR3148/RAND_RR3148.pdf) · [ISCRR](https://www.iscrr.com.au/news/newsletter/july-2023-ova/)

> A brand that transparently grounds content in the actual evidence, **measures outcomes** (restraint reduction, incident rates, Code Black activations, staff confidence), and publishes results would own a claim **nobody credibly holds today** — and it maps directly onto Dromaios's own "evidence-led, claims kept close to proof" value. *In this category, being credible IS the moat.*

**Other open lanes:** dementia-specific de-escalation (Dementia Australia's VR "D-Esc" validates demand but is grant-funded only to 30 Jun 2026 — a paid-market vacuum opens after); demonstrated competency assessment (vs passive video); dual aged-care **+** NDIS audit-ready compliance mapping in one product; and transparent published pricing (everyone is quote-gated).

## 5. STREAM 4 — Medtech (the long game) — hard regulatory facts

The TGA regulates on **intended purpose**, not the presence of AI. Stay on the right side of these lines:

- **Three buckets:** *Excluded* (not regulated — simple admin/record-keeping), *Exempt* (is a device but not ARTG-listed — e.g. qualifying CDSS), *Included* (ARTG + conformity assessment).
- **CDSS exemption — all three must hold:** (1) sole purpose is to support a recommendation to a *health professional*; (2) does **not** process a medical image/signal from another device; (3) does **not** replace clinical judgement. Even exempt CDSS must **notify the TGA within 30 working days** of supply (Therapeutic Goods (Medical Devices) Regs 2002, Sch 4 Pt 2). [TGA CDSS](https://www.tga.gov.au/resources/guidance/understanding-clinical-decision-support-system-software-regulation)
- **AI scribes:** *not* regulated **if** confined to record-keeping/summarisation — but if they suggest diagnoses/treatments they become medical devices needing ARTG approval. TGA has flagged an **urgent review** of digital scribes (assume the carve-out tightens). [TGA digital scribes](https://www.tga.gov.au/products/medical-devices/software-and-artificial-intelligence/manufacturing/artificial-intelligence-ai-and-medical-device-software/digital-scribes)
- **AHPRA obligations apply even to unregulated software** (Aug 2024 guidance): practitioner accountability, understanding of the tool, transparency + informed consent. AI scribes require informing patients and obtaining consent. [AHPRA](https://www.ahpra.gov.au/Resources/Artificial-Intelligence-in-healthcare.aspx)

> **Strategic read:** Keep ClinicBoss and any HIL tool *deliberately* on the Excluded/Exempt side (operations, documentation, compliance evidence — not diagnosis), write a tight intended-purpose statement, build human-in-the-loop + consent + AU data residency in from day one. This both de-risks regulatorily and *is* the "responsible medtech, earned" brand.

## 8. The Path to #1 — prioritised playbook

### Quick wins (0–6 months) — cheap, high-leverage, build proof
1. **Plant the "evidence-led de-escalation" flag.** Publish an honest, cited review of the de-escalation evidence gap (using the RAND/EDITION/ISCRR sources above) and commit publicly to measuring outcomes. This is content, credibility, and category-definition in one move — and it's *on-brand* (claims close to proof).
2. **Own the NDIS + Strengthened-Standards content cluster.** A continuously-updated, definitive AU resource hub (mandatory-training requirements, SIRS 24-hr rules, Action 2.9.6, NDIS Practice Standards, RACGP 6th-ed migration). Capture the intent no product brand owns.
3. **Launch a free provider-owner community (HIL/Skool).** Top-of-funnel + design partners + review flywheel. Costs little; compounds.
4. **Transparent flat pricing as a public stance** — directly contrast Halaxy credits / Nookal per-seat escalation (internally; soften for public per guardrails).
5. **Build a review-generation habit** from the first pilots — the whole market's review counts are low; rank fast.

### Medium-term moats (6–18 months) — build the wedge product
6. **ClinicBoss = compliance-native PMS for the aged-care/NDIS-allied convergence.** The differentiator is **continuous audit-readiness baked into daily workflow** (auto-link training completions, incidents, policies to specific Standard outcomes) + **guaranteed one-click data export** (weaponising the Helix/MediRecords export-failure complaints) + **AU data residency** + **human AU-timezone support** (the #1 complaint everywhere).
7. **A reliable, compliance-grade AI documentation tool (HIL)** that writes NDIS/allied-health notes *with the funding justification* — the uncontested vertical Heidi/Lyrebird ignore — and that stays deliberately on the TGA Excluded/Exempt side.
8. **Dual-sector audit-ready compliance mapping** (aged care **+** NDIS in one product) — for the many providers reporting to both commissions.
9. **A measured de-escalation outcome study** (partner a university/ISCRR-type body) — converts the #1 flag into defensible proof and a reference.

### Long-term defensible advantages (18 months+) — the durable #1
10. **The fused system: training + compliance + operations as one evidence-linked source of truth**, sequenced as *lead with evidence-led de-escalation (#1) → wrap in AU-sovereign data/AI (#3) → expand into unified compliance/training evidence-trail (#2)*. Competitors are structurally siloed (content-volume LMS, ops-first care-management, GP-centric scribes) and **cannot easily follow** without rebuilding their model.
11. **Data network effect:** the AU benchmark dataset (utilisation, incidents, claim turnaround, de-escalation outcomes) becomes proprietary content, a sales tool, and a research asset no competitor can replicate.
12. **Responsible-medtech credibility** as the brand: the slow, evidence-first path *is* the moat in a sector where "build trust before grand claims" is the documented procurement filter (cf. CancerAid, ANDHealth's de-risking thesis). [ANDHealth](https://www.andhealth.com.au/)
